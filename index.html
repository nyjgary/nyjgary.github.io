Hello! I am a recent MBA graduate from The University of Chicago, currently working in management consulting and looking to transition into data science. I have been ramping up my knowledge on R, Python, and data science through a variety of online resources. Below is a collection of projects that chart my journey and showcase skills I've have learnt.

Peeking into the world of P2P lending with Prosper data

An exploratory data analysis of Prosper's 2005-2014 loan-level data, this study seeks to explore the characteristics of typical borrowers, nature of loans originated, and growth trajectory of the nation's first peer-to-peer lending marketplace. This project is coded in R and employs heavy usage of ggplot2, as well as other R packages like maps, dplyr, and reshape2. Check it out!

Investigating Titanic passengers' survival rates

My first initiation into the world of NumPy and Pandas, this is a simple analysis of Titanic passenger data, coded in IPython. Through graphical plots and summary statistics, this study explores variables affecting the likelihood of surviving the tragedy, including age, gender as well as other less obvious factors.
